TYPES

Types of Shochu & Awamori

A world of diverse flavors shaped by ingredients and koji

🍠
Imo (Sweet Potato) Shochu
芋焼酎
The signature spirit of Kagoshima and Miyazaki. Varieties like Kogane Sengan and purple sweet potato each bring distinct character. Best enjoyed with hot water to unlock the aroma.
🌾
Mugi (Barley) Shochu
麦焼酎
Oita and Nagasaki lead production. Clean, light, and approachable. Excellent on the rocks or with soda.
🍚
Kome (Rice) Shochu
米焼酎
Kumamoto's Kuma shochu is the benchmark. Elegant sweetness and ginjo-like aromatics that echo the refinement of sake.
🟫
Kokuto (Brown Sugar) Shochu
黒糖焼酎
Exclusively produced on the Amami Islands. Sweet, aromatic nose yet zero sugar in the final spirit. Try it on the rocks.
🌺
Awamori
泡盛
Okinawa's 600-year-old distilling tradition. Made entirely with black koji and Thai indica rice. Kusu (aged 3+ years) is transcendent.
🥣
Soba (Buckwheat) & More
そば・その他
Buckwheat shochu, kasutori (sake lees) shochu, chestnut shochu, and beyond. Regional specialties that showcase Japan's distilling diversity.
